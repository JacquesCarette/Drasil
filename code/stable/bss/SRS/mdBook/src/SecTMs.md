|Label      |Center-of-mass constraint                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
|Equation   |\\[\displaystyle\sum\_{i=1}^{n}{m\_{i}\\,{\boldsymbol{p}\text{(}t\text{)}}\_{i}}=0\\]\\[{m\_{1}}\\,{{x\_{1}}^{0}}+{m\_{2}}\\,{{x\_{2}}^{0}}=0\\]\\[{m\_{1}}\\,{{y\_{1}}^{0}}+{m\_{2}}\\,{{y\_{2}}^{0}}=0\\]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|Description|<ul><li>\\(m\\) is the mass (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\(i\\) is the index (Unitless)</li><li>\\(\boldsymbol{p}\text{(}t\text{)}\\) is the position (\\({\text{m}}\\))</li></ul><ul><li>\\({m\_{1}}\\) is the mass of the first star (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\({{x\_{1}}^{0}}\\) is the initial x-position of the first star (\\({\text{m}}\\))</li><li>\\({m\_{2}}\\) is the mass of the second star (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\({{x\_{2}}^{0}}\\) is the initial x-position of the second star (\\({\text{m}}\\))</li></ul><ul><li>\\({m\_{1}}\\) is the mass of the first star (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\({{y\_{1}}^{0}}\\) is the initial y-position of the first star (\\({\text{m}}\\))</li><li>\\({m\_{2}}\\) is the mass of the second star (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\({{y\_{2}}^{0}}\\) is the initial y-position of the second star (\\({\text{m}}\\))</li></ul>|
|Notes      |<ul><li>The first equation gives the general center-of-mass constraint for a system of \\(n\\) point masses, where \\(i\\) indexes each body. In the center-of-mass reference frame ([A:inertialFrame](./SecAssumps.md#inertialFrame)), the origin is chosen so that the mass-weighted sum of positions vanishes. The remaining equations specialize to the binary case (n = 2), decomposing the vector constraint into \\(x\\)-direction and \\(y\\)-direction components of the initial positions.</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |

## Newton's law of universal gravitation {#TM:UniversalGravLaw}

</div>

|Refname    |TM:UniversalGravLaw                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
|:----------|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Label      |Newton's law of universal gravitation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
|Equation   |\\[{\boldsymbol{F}\_{\boldsymbol{g}}}=G\\,\frac{{m\_{1}}\\,{m\_{2}}}{{r\_{12}}^{2}}\\]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
|Description|<ul><li>\\({\boldsymbol{F}\_{\boldsymbol{g}}}\\) is the force of gravity (\\({\text{N}}\\))</li><li>\\(G\\) is the gravitational constant (\\(\frac{\text{m}^{3}}{\text{kg}\text{s}^{2}}\\))</li><li>\\({m\_{1}}\\) is the mass of the first star (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\({m\_{2}}\\) is the mass of the second star (\\({\text{kg}}\\))</li><li>\\({r\_{12}}\\) is the separation distance (\\({\text{m}}\\))</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                                             |
|Notes      |<ul><li>\\({\boldsymbol{F}\_{\boldsymbol{g}}}\\) is the magnitude of the gravitational force between two bodies; it is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. The force acts along the relative-position vector ([TM:relPosAndSep](./SecTMs.md#TM:relPosAndSep)), directed from one body toward the other. This assumes Newtonian gravitation ([A:newtonianGravity](./SecAssumps.md#newtonianGravity)) and requires the separation distance to be positive ([A:nonzeroSeparation](./SecAssumps.md#nonzeroSeparation)).</li></ul>|
